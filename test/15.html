Comment 11 by uahgekido, Sep 02, 2008 
Rather than post what I had originally typed out, which mirrored many opinions 
already posted, I'll offer a little note to keep in mind.

Mozilla specifies a nearly identical set of build requirements for Firefox 
(difference: they do support VS2008 in the Firefox 3 branch) as Google. Does this 
settle the argument? No, not at all. But Firefox is one of the most well-known OSS 
projects in the world, and they seem to have gotten along fine with this requirement.  
Even though Google (and Mozilla) may specify build instructions that require VS, a 
developer is not *required* to use that. The developer is more than welcome to 
attempt to adapt the code base into something that is compilable in an open-source 
environment on Windows.

However, there could be numerous reasons why VS and the Windows SDK to develop on 
Windows. These would likely be the exact same reasons that Xcode is required for the 
Mac build. (sure, Xcode is free, but you do still go through the EULAs, which is the 
argument after all)

Comment 17 by russell.jones, Sep 07, 2008 
"Needing to use a non-open-source compiler to build Chromium does not make Chromium 
non-open-source.  Sorry."
This is correct.

The question of whether it is desirable to use an OSS compiler on Windows is another
issue. Whilst I think the OSS development process is superior, morally and very often
practically especially over the longer term, I believe the compilers' use is not
desirable here, as it makes it too easy for MS to introduce incompatibilities (via
Windows Update or new OS versions) and then dismiss breakage by pointing out that the
compiler is unsupported. I strongly suspect one reason they have experts in competing
technologies to facilitate exactly this. In contrast, if they break their own
compiler their customers (the ones they care about, I mean) will not be happy and
they will be under strong pressure to fix it.
